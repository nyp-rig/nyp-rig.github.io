Robotics Timetable

Date Theme Description
8th August Learning Basics of Microbit Our Robotics CCA team will be exploring the basics of Micro:bit radio communication, focusing on how to create simple transmitter and receiver systems using code. All taught by our professional coach.
During this session, members will:
  • Learn how to use Micro:bit's radio feature to send and receive signals wirelessly.
  • Program one Micro:bit as a transmitter and another as a receiver.
Implement actions such as:
  • Pressing Button A to transmit one icon (e.g., a smiley face).
  • Pressing Button B to transmit a different icon (e.g., a heart).
  • Tilting the Micro:bit to trigger and send a third icon.
15th August Learn to assemble the robot car and program it to be controlled with a Micro:bit. Our Robotics CCA will be hosting a hands-on session where members will work in teams to design, build, and program their own remote-controlled cars using the Maqueen Pro robot car kits.
22th August Pick & Place Challenge Using the servo motor Micro:bit kit, make a robotic arm or gripper to pick up objects (like bottle caps or blocks) and place them in a target zone. This will train student's precision timing and position sensing.
29th August Warehouse Delivery Simulation Use the forklift bot to navigate a track to pick up and deliver "packages" to assigned drop zones. Then, we will attempt to write a program for the bot to move autonomously. This will train students ability to do basic robot control using a tank drive control system (or our experimental arcade drive system), and train students for autonomous programming.
5th September Smart Navigation Using IR Sensors Learn how IR sensors detect black lines on white surfaces! We will program the Maqueen Pro to follow a line track by writing and adjusting the PID (proportional-integral-derivative) control loop logic for smooth navigation.
We will also compete in a "race track" line-following challenge.
12th September Sumo Bot - Push Blade Edition Build: Attach the pusher blade Task: Compete to push opponents out of the ring. 🎯 Learn about: Power control Strategic design and defensive mechanics
19th September Disaster Rescue Simulation Build: Use a gripper or claw Task: Pick up "injured persons" (soft objects) and transport them to safety zones 🎯 Learn about: Robotics in real-world disaster scenarios Controlled movement + object interaction
26th September 🚦 Smart Car with Traffic Light Detection using AI Lens Kit Program your robot to use the AI Smart Lens to see traffic lights, recognize the color, and decide whether to stop or move — just like a real self-driving car!
3rd October Robotics Club Creative Open House Members choose their own mini project or combine everything they've learned Ideas:
  1. A robot pet that reacts to touch/sound
  2. A delivery robot with obstacle avoidance
  3. A musical car that plays a tone when moving
  4. Showcase and short presentations by each team
  5. A cleaning robot with obstacle avoidance and a touch navigation menu for users to navigate around NYP would help NYP keep the school clean.
